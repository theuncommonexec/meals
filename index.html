🫓 Monday batch — Latin / Venezuelan

Meat 1: Instant Pot kalua pork
Cube pork shoulder, layer with a few slices of bacon and coarse (Hawaiian) sea salt. Pressure cook 35 min high, natural release, then shred. Cook shredded cabbage in the smoky broth if you like.
Recipe → Nom Nom Paleo
Meat 2: Beef picadillo — doubles for Taco Tuesday
Brown ground beef, add a sofrito of onion, garlic and pepper, then tomato, cumin, and a few olives/capers. Simmer to a savory mince. Save half for Tuesday's tacos.
Recipe → Laylita
Fish: Pan-seared salmon with chimichurri
Sear salmon in olive oil, skin-side down, until crisp. Top with chimichurri (parsley, oregano, garlic, red pepper, vinegar, oil) — kept savory for Tom.
Chimichurri → Laylita
Seafood: Baked gambas al ajillo (garlic shrimp)
Toss shrimp with lots of sliced garlic, olive oil, a pinch of chili and parsley; bake hot until just pink. Quick and savory.
Recipe → Laylita
Veggie 1: Sautéed green beans with garlic & tomato
Sauté green beans with garlic, add chopped tomato, cook until tender-crisp; season with salt.
Veggie 2: Tostones (savory fried green plantains)
Slice green plantains, fry, smash flat, fry again until crisp; salt. Serve with a garlic dip.
Side/carb: Corn fritters — torrejas de choclo
Blend fresh corn with egg, a little flour and cheese; pan-fry small fritters until golden.
Recipe → Laylita
Breakfast 1: Arepas with egg & avocado
Mix masarepa, water and salt; form patties, griddle then finish in the oven. Split and fill with scrambled egg and avocado.
Breakfast 2: Banana-almond-cocoa bread
Mash banana, whisk with eggs, almond flour, cocoa, honey and baking soda; bake ~40 min. Low-sugar family favorite, batches well.
🌮 Taco Tuesday: picadillo / salmon tacos
Warm quality corn tortillas; fill with beef picadillo (or flaked salmon), top with guacamole and pico de gallo. Reuses Monday's meat & fish.
Taco side: Guacamole (make)
Mash avocado with lime juice; mix in diced onion, jalapeño/serrano, crushed garlic, chopped cilantro and salt. Leave a few chunks.
Recipe → Laylita
Taco side: Pico de gallo (make)
Finely dice tomato, onion, cilantro and jalapeño; toss with lime juice and salt. Let it sit a few minutes before serving. (No dedicated recipe on your saved sites — it's a quick chop-and-mix.)
Taco side: Tortilla chips (buy)
Store-bought — grab a good-quality bag of corn tortilla chips.

🥡 Thursday batch — Chinese

Meat 1: Three cup chicken (san bei ji)
Brown bone-in chicken pieces, then braise with roughly equal "cups" of sesame oil, soy sauce and rice wine plus ginger, garlic and a little sugar. Reduce to a glaze and finish with lots of Thai basil.
Recipe → Red House Spice
Meat 2: Instant Pot hong shao pork with daikon & carrot
Blanch pork (belly or shoulder). Pressure cook 25 min high with Shaoxing wine, light + dark soy, ginger, star anise and bay; natural release. Switch to Sauté, add daikon, carrot and rock sugar, simmer ~10–12 min until tender and glossy.
Base recipe → Red House Spice
Fish: Steamed fish, Chinese style (清蒸鱼)
Steam a whole fish or fillets with ginger and scallion until just cooked. Top with fresh scallion and cilantro, pour over a light soy dressing, then sizzle hot oil on top. Clean and savory.
Recipe → Red House Spice
Veggie 1: Stir-fried gai lan (Chinese broccoli)
Blanch gai lan briefly, then stir-fry with garlic and finish with oyster sauce or a splash of soy.
Veggie 2: Carrot, celery, mushroom & wood-ear stir-fry (meatless)
Stir-fry carrot and celery in garlic oil, add mushroom and soaked wood ear, then a sauce of light + dark soy, oyster sauce, cornstarch, pepper, a pinch of sugar and sesame oil. Toss until glossy; finish with scallion.
Base recipe (meatless) → Red House Spice
Combined: Soy-sauce chow mein
Boil noodles, then stir-fry with your choice of protein and greens in a soy-sauce mix. The kids' favorite.
Recipe → Red House Spice
Breakfast 1: Whole-wheat scallion pancakes
Make a soft dough, coil with scallions and a little oil, roll flat and pan-fry until crisp. Batch and freeze; reheat in a pan.
Breakfast 2: Banana overnight oats
Combine oats, milk, mashed banana and cinnamon; chill overnight. Top with soft fruit (berries) before serving.
